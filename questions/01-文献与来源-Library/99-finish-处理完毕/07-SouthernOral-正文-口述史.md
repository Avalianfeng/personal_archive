Initial Interview Questions:
This biographical interview should provide the basic framework for your conversations with each respondent
interviewed. In this initial interview, respondents narrate the story of their lives, emphasizing the events and
experiences which seem most important and meaningful to them. This interview will allow you to discover
the respondent’s paths of association regarding life events and the setting, timing, sequence, and duration of
these events. It will enable you to obtain unanticipated information as well as answers to many of the
questions which interest you without imposing your own perspective. It will help you formulate subsequent
questions within the respondent’s own frame of reference.
Although this interview does not follow a rigid schedule of questions, it should not be unstructured. It should
proceed in rough chronological order, though respondents may not necessarily arrange their memories with
dates as benchmarks. You should guide the course of the conversation by asking for elaboration or
clarification, bringing up topics which the respondent has not thought to mention and asking exploratory
questions in order to discover the respondent’s areas of special knowledge.
Did you know your grandparents? What did they do? Establish any background the respondent might have about the
origins of their family.
Where was your first home place? Where had your parents come from?
When did you come here? Why? With whom? What neighborhood/community did you live in at first?
How many people lived in your home? Did you, your parents, and your siblings all live together? Did anyone
besides immediate family live with you? Did relatives live nearby? How often did your household interact with
relatives or close friends?
Before you left home, did your family change residence? Why, where to, and how often?
Where did you attend school? For how long? (Ask for brothers and sisters.)
Did your family attend church / temple? Which church / temple and where?
Did you have any jobs or particular responsibilities as a child?
When did you leave home? Why?
What was your first job? How long did you stay at this work? What other jobs have you held? For how long? When
did you retire?
View of working life; did you enjoy working?
Did you marry? How did you meet your spouse? What jobs did he/she do? Have you married more than once?
How often have you moved since you left home? How long did you stay in each place?
Are your parents still alive? How long ago did your parents pass away? Who takes/took care of them in their old
age?
What were things like for your family during the Depression? How did the Depression affect your community?

Detailed Life History Questions: Family History
This more detailed list of life history questions focuses on family genealogy, family rituals and relationships,
and household routines.
Family Background & Immigration:
Were you, your parents, or grandparents born outside of the U.S.? If yes, where were you / they born and how long
did they live there?
When and why did your family decide to move to the United States?
Do you remember hearing family members describe what life was like in their homeland? What stories did they tell?
Do you have old family photographs, letters, antiques, or other memorabilia from your homeland? What special
memories or stories are assoicated with them? How did you you acquire them?
Do you remember family stories about the frustrations and difficulties of adjusting to life in the United States? Were
there family stories about the benefits of life in the United States?
If your family members did not speak English before arriving in the United States, when / how did they learn to
speak English?
What language(s) did your family members speak in their homeland? Do you or your family members still speak in
that language? When do you use the language of your homeland and when do you use English? What determines
which language you use?
Do your family’s children and / or grandchildren who were born in the U.S. speak the homeland’s language? Why or
why not?
Do you feel that you or your family members have ever been discriminated against because you were from another
country? How did you respond?
Do you still have relatives in your family’s homeland? Do you stay in contact with them? In what ways?
Did you or other family members ever make a return visit to your family’s homeland? What was the reason for the
visit? How long did you stay? What happened during your visit?

Household Organization and Domestic Routine:
What do you remember about your grandparents? Where did they live? Did you see them often? What would you do
with them? Did they ever talk to you about their youth? What occupations did they engage in?
What other relatives were you close to besides your immediate family: aunts, uncles, cousins? Did these relatives
live with you? Relationships with separated relatives?
Was there any person in your family older than you who you were especially close to? What impact did this
person’s aging and death have on you?
Who were the members of your household? Any boarders? (Follow up with questions about the role of extended
family members within the household.)
What were the relationships between parents and children like when you were growing? Did you want to or not want
to repeat these patterns with your own children?
What do you remember about your home? What did it look like? What do you remember about your home’s layout,
rooms, furnishings, artwork and portraits? Did your home have outbuildings? When you were at home, where did
you and your family spend the most time? What were the sleeping arrangements in your home? Did you have indoor
plumbing? Was your home owned or rented?
Who were your neighbors? How far away were your neighbors? Did relatives live nearby? Which relatives?
Who took responsibility for various household chores: cleaning, washing, cooking, gardening, sewing, child care
(bathing, bedtime, etc.)?
Describe the meals the family took together. Describe your family’s diet. What foods were commonly served? What
were meal time seating arrangements? What kind of discussions occurred at meal time?
Was religious training important in the home?
What part did religion play in family life? What was a typical Sunday like? Did your family attend church / temple?
Describe Mass / Services.
Describe holidays: Christmas, Hanukkah, Thanksgiving, Fourth of July, etc.
What do you recall about weddings, baptisms, funerals, and other family rituals?
Describe your family’s most important traditions. Who started these? (Grandparents? Or were they started after
their death?) Who enjoyed the ceremonies most?
Describe funerals in your family. Were stories told during the funerals? What were some of them?
What kinds of things would your family do together? Family entertainments?
What family stories, sayings, principles, and codes of conduct stand out in your mind?
Did men and women do things together? Separately?
Did your family ever move? Why? How often? How did this affect you?
What do you remember about the care of babies in your home?
What about care of the elderly? What was done for them? Who did it?
Who took care of you when you were ill? Who generally cared for the sick in your family?
What procedure was followed? What were the effects of disease on your family?
When your parents were both away from home, who cared for the children?

Detailed Life History Questions: Community History
This selection of the interview questions explores the interaction of the individual with the community.
Education:
Did you go to school (elementary, high school, college, graduate school)? Who encouraged / discouraged you?
Why?
Where did you go to school as a child? How did you get there? Describe the school building.
How long did you expect to stay in school when you started? Did you want to stay in school longer than you did?
How long did you go to school? Why did you leave?
What were the major differences in your education and that of your parents, your children?
Did you learn to read and write in school? At home? When? If not, how has this affected your work? Your life?
What do you remember about your teachers? Were you ever disciplined by your teachers? Was this fair? Did the
teachers play favorites? Why? Who were the favored students?
What did you like about school? What didn’t you like?

Politics:
Did your parents / you belong to any political organizations? Do you remember attending these meetings with your

Who were the most powerful members of your community? Did these people have both political and economic

Do you remember any particular controversy within your community? How was it settled? What were your

Who were your town / city council people? Did they change over time?

What influence did local companies have on politics?
Do you remember any political rallies or marches?
Neighborhood, Community, Leisure:
Define your neighborhood or community. Describe your neighborhood’s geographic boundaries.
Who lived / lives in your neighborhood? Did you work with them? Have your neighbors changed much over the

How many people did you know well in your neighborhood?
Who were your parents’ closest friends? Did they do the same work as your parents?
Where were the gathering places in town? Clubs? Recreational or fun places? Where did men / women / blacks /

Were there particular places you liked to spend time as a child and adolescent? As an adult?
Was there a bad part of town? Were you afraid to go there? Describe what it looked like.
Where did your parents do their shopping? Name some of the stores in your town. What were their functions? Did

Where did you do your shopping as an adult? Today?
What were the main voluntary associations in town (KKK, Kiwanis, Masons, Order of the Red Men)?

Detailed Life History:
Occupational History Questions for Workers
The following questions were developed for interviews conducted with Piedmont industrial laborers.
Interviewers speaking with other kinds of workers, especially professional workers, will need to modify some
of the topics and questions to make them more appropriate to their respondents’ backgrounds and
experiences.
Hiring, Training, Performance, Conditions:
When did you, your brothers, your sisters, first start working outside of the home? Why?
How did you find your first job? Do you remember your first day of work?
Who taught you how to perform your job? Was it difficult to learn?
Describe what you did. How quickly did you have to work? Did you have a production quota? What were your
hours and how were they recorded?
What were your feelings about the work that you did?
Did you learn to take short-cuts in performing this work?
Was the work dangerous? Were you afraid of getting hurt? Was anyone injured on the job?
What were sanitary conditions on the job (air quality, smells, noise, heat, etc.)?
Was there a speed-up or stretch out at your plant? Describe.
Were you assisted in your work by other employees? Could you help out a fellow worker if you were finished with
your job?
Were your fellow employees mostly male / female? Black, white, Hispanic?
Supervision:
How closely were you supervised in your work? Did you need supervision? Did supervisors or managers ever try to
make you work more quickly? Did they ever try to study how fast you could work?
Were there certain rules at the workplace (smoking, eating, talking, breaks, dress, behavior with management)?
Did you have to be at work on time? What happened if you weren’t? What holidays could you take? Did you ever
take off from work without the supervisor’s permission? Why? For how long? Could you get your job back after you
returned?
Did you and other workers ever decide among yourselves how the work should be done and how much work each
person would do? Did each individual abide by the rules? Could you deliberately slow down production? What
might happen?
Could you and your fellow employees ever make up rules that had to be followed by other workers or supervisors?
Did you ever complain to your supervisor about problems related to your work? About other workers? About
excessive supervision? How did they respond? How were other workers’ grievances settled?
Was there any competition among workers? Did management promote competition? How were promotions
arranged? Were you ever promoted to higher positions? Were you ever overlooked for promotion? Why? How did
you respond?
Do you remember any conflicts or jealousies between different groups of workers (skilled vs. unskilled, black vs.
white, old vs. new, etc.)? Were some workers treated with more respect than others? Why and by whom?

Working Women:
Were female workers treated differently than male workers? Why and in what ways?
Did supervisors play favorites among the women? Why?
Did families worry about their daughters being exploited by supervisors? Did you ever observe this kind of
exploitation in your workplace?
For female workers:
What did your parents / other workers think about your going to work?
Did you plan to keep working after you got married? Did you quit or lose your job or take time off when you had
your first baby?
How did your husband feel about your working outside of the home?
When and why did you go back to work after leaving for any reason?
Who helped with the housework when you were working outside of the home?
What did you do with your wages?
Race / Ethnicity:
Did your workplace employ people of different races / ethnicities?
Did people of different races / ethnicities perform the same kinds of work? Why or why not?
Did employees of different ethnic / racial groups spend time together when they weren’t working (eat lunch
together, take work breaks together, play on the same company sports teams, etc.)?
Was there ever any tension or disagreement between workers or different races / ethnicities? When did this occur
and why? Was there any effort on the part of workers or employers to ease the tension? Why? How?
The Company:
What were you paid for your work? How were you paid?
What did you think of the company? What benefits did the company offer? Insurance? Medical care? Recreation?
Educational activities?
Did the company always keep its word? How or how not?
Social Activities:
Did any of your co-workers live nearby? What kinds of activities would you do with fellow employees outside of
work? Could you socialize with supervisors?
Were there company-sponsored recreational activities (sports teams, singing groups, volunteer groups, etc)?
Describe them.
Was participation voluntary? Did the company offer prizes or awards for participation? For winning?
If there were organized athletic teams in the plant, did you have rivalries with other departments in the plant? With
other companies? With other towns?
Do you remember any changes in the company’s recreational program during the years you worked in industry? Did
the changes make working in the company more or less enjoyable or did it make little difference? Why?

Were company-organized recreational and sports activities a topic of conversation at work?
Did the union ever organize a sports team?
Mobility:
Did you change jobs during during your working years? If yes, why did you change jobs? Describe these jobs and
compare them. If not, why not? Did you ever dream of doing other kinds of work?
Organization:
Did you ever join a union? Why or why not? What were the circumstances?
How was your local union organized? What was your role in the union?
How did the union educate workers about its roles and goals? In your opinion, was this education useful /
successful? Why or why not?
How important was the union in your daily life? Did the union sponsor social events?
Did you have a shop steward? What role did he / she play in settling grievances? Did your local union help or
disappoint you personally? How much contact did you have with the national? Did you go to national conventions?
Who were the principal organizers in your local? Officials?
Were you or other workers ever harassed because of union membership?
Did the union ever go out on strike? Why? Describe the strike and your participation in it.
Who led the strike? Men? Women? Skilled or unskilled labor?
How did people in the community (preachers, store owners, neighbors, etc.) react to the strike? How did the police
react? What was management’s reaction?
What broke the strike? What was the impact?
What was the overall effect of the union on the company? On you? On your family?
Were you aware of other strikes around the region?
Feelings about Work:
Were you proud of or embarrassed by the work you did? Why?
What did you think of the company’s product(s)?
What did you like about your work? What did you dislike? Did you look forward to each new day?
What is the best thing that has happened to working people in your lifetime?
What was the worst thing that happened?
Did you ever wish you didn’t have to work? When? Why?
What do you think about welfare, food stamps, unemployment insurance?
Do you do any kind of work in your retirement? What? How does it compare with the work you did years ago?
